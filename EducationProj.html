Throughout 2016 and 2017, I would teach STEAM (STEM + art and design) workshops for free at public places like the Fremont Main Library and the East Bay School for Boys. They emphasized creative coding. I did it because I really wanted students to see early on that math can be artsy, coding can be entertaining, and art has its geometry. I had the privilege of teaching kids as young as 5 to high school students everything from how to code recursive fractals to and how to program minimalist Mario games.
Prior to my trip to Peru, I developed a curriculum that is a beginner's guide to makerspaces (en Espanol). These Powerpoints were my lesson plans, but they were not how we learned. Everyone learned through debugging and discussion, through Google Translate and helpful teachers.

To help them crystallize their knowledge into projects, we had the students help with the mouth mouse and a water conductivity sensor.
