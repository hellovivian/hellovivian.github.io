Volunteer

Girls in Engineering
Summer 2018

Purpose

This summer, I learned about the Open Prosthetics initiative as I became a volunteer workshop presenter for Girls in Engineering.

Process

Throughout June, I learned how to use the Ultimaker series to print the parts to a Raptor hand. Using engineering diagrams, I then pieced together the articulated hand. In July, I presented on Open Prosthetics to dozens of girls from the ages of 10 to 17, and helped them assemble hands as well. I was also there to present and share some of my own work, which tends to involve both art and engineering.

Reflection

Hands are really beautiful things. The dexterity that comes from their 27 degrees of freedom is stunning. As I learned in a course on bio-inspired design once, nature is an incredible tinkerer. But engineering is wonderful too, and the fact that we can replicate the functionality of hands through digital fabrication is a testament to that.



I was really honored that Girls in Engineering let me speak and share about myself.
